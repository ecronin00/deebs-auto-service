Understanding State Vehicle Inspections

In Maryland, a state vehicle inspection is required under specific circumstances to ensure that vehicles meet established safety standards. These inspections are crucial for maintaining road safety and minimizing environmental impact.

When You Need a Maryland State Inspection:

  1. Vehicle Sale or Transfer: In Maryland, a state inspection certificate is required when a vehicle is sold or ownership is transferred. The seller or transferor must obtain the inspection certificate.
  2. Registration of Out-of-State Vehicles: If you're bringing a vehicle into Maryland from another state, you must pass the Maryland State Inspection to register the vehicle.
  3. Periodic Inspections for Commercial Vehicles: Certain commercial vehicles in Maryland may be subject to periodic inspections.

  • Steering and Alignment: Checking for proper steering performance and wheel alignment.
  • Lights and Electrical Systems: Ensuring that headlights, taillights, turn signals, and other electrical systems are operational.
  • Windows and Mirrors: Verifying the integrity and clarity of windows, windshield, and mirrors.
  • Exhaust System: Checking the exhaust system for leaks or damages and ensuring it meets emission standards.
  • Tires, Wheels, and Fuel System: Inspecting tires for tread depth and wear, wheels for integrity, and the fuel system for leaks.
  • Safety Equipment: Verifying the presence and functionality of safety equipment like seat belts and airbags.

After the Inspection:

  • Passing the Inspection: If your vehicle passes, you'll receive a certificate that you can use for vehicle registration or sale.
  • Failing the Inspection: If your vehicle fails, you'll need to have the necessary repairs made and then have the vehicle re-inspected.

It's important to note that Maryland State Inspections are carried out by authorized inspection stations throughout the state. These inspections are thorough to ensure that vehicles on Maryland roads are safe and environmentally compliant.
