Pad, Rotor, and Fluid Replacements

Learn the Brake-sics

Brake Pads

Brake pads create the friction needed to slow and stop the vehicle.

Brake Rotors

Brake rotors are discs that brake pads clamp down on to halt wheel spin.

Brake Fluid

Brake fluid transmits pedal force into braking power.

When to Replace Brake Pads

Mileage

Typically every 50,000 miles

Warning Signs
  • Squealing or grinding noises
  • Longer stopping distances
  • Vibration in the steering wheel
  • Pad thickness < 1/4 inch

When to Replace Rotors

Mileage

Every 30,000 to 70,000 miles

Warning Signs
  • Pulsating brake pedal
  • Visible scoring or grooves
  • Unusual noises during braking

What Does Brake Fluid Even Do?

Brake fluid transfers force from the brake pedal to the brakes for effective stopping. Over time, it absorbs moisture, reducing braking efficiency and risking damage to the braking system. Regularly replacing brake fluid helps maintain safe brake function.
