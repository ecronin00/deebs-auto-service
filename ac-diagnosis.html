Here's what could be wrong

Refrigerant Leak

The most common AC issue, where refrigerant escapes due to system leaks, reducing the system's cooling capacity. Repairs may involve sealing leaks and recharging refrigerant, costing between $200 and $1000.

Faulty Compressor

The compressor pressurizes the refrigerant and propels it through the AC system; its failure means no cooling. Replacement, including labor, can cost around $1,100 (according to JDPower) including labor.

Electrical Issues

Electrical faults can include blown fuses, relay problems, or faulty wiring, all of which disrupt the power supply to the AC system. Repair costs including labor can range from $150 to $900.

Clogged Fitler

This is what Jiffy Lube tries to sell everyone with an oil change. The cabin air filter cleans incoming air; when clogged, it restricts airflow and diminishes AC efficiency. Replacement costs, including labor, are generally between $20 to $100.

*Note that prices may vary depending on the severity of the issue, the vehicle's make and model, and the region. It is advisable to get a personalized quote from DEEBS AUTO SERVICE.
